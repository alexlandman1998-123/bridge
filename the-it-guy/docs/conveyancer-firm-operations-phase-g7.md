# Conveyancer firm operations — Phase G7

Phase G7 gives a firm manager a governed contract for configuring and supervising conveyancing operations without developer intervention. It remains provider-independent and does not persist, notify, assign, generate documents or change legal outcomes by itself.

## Delivered configuration

- Versioned branches, departments and teams with referential integrity.
- Role capability profiles that may narrow, but never expand, the G1 authority ceiling.
- Approval thresholds and separation-of-duty requirements.
- Delegation limits that exclude G1 non-delegable powers.
- Matter-type playbooks bound to exact plan and exception-library versions.
- SLA and escalation policies bound to working calendars and holidays.
- Exact template and clause-pack version assignments.
- Queue-priority weights and role/team capacity rules.

Published configurations are immutable snapshots. A new revision must name the immediately preceding configuration and fingerprint. Existing matters retain their original binding. G7 can compare revisions and produce an explicit adoption preview, but it never adopts the change or rewrites the matter.

## Delivered operational intelligence

- Access-filtered global matter search.
- Explainable firm supervisory queue based on configured blocker, overdue and aging weights.
- Member and team capacity projections that complement A6 action ownership.
- Aging and blocked-matter reporting.
- Expected-registration forecasts based only on an explicit matter estimate or configured duration.
- Staff indicators based on visible matter counts and target dates.
- Fee, direct-cost and profitability projections in integer minor units, explicitly outside trust accounting.

Every result declares its methodology and remains read-only. Registration forecasts are operational estimates, not predictions of a legal or Deeds Office outcome.

## Exit gate

The contract satisfies G7 when a tenant-bound firm manager can publish a valid practice configuration, inspect revision impact, preserve active-matter history and obtain access-controlled operational supervision projections without a developer changing code. Application persistence, manager screens and orchestration are separate productisation concerns.
